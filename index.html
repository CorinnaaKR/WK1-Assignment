Panda Facts

  • Panda poo, because it is primarily made up of bamboo, can be made into paper!
  • Giant Pandas are native to China and primarily live in bamboo forests.
  • Giant Pandas are excellent climbers and can reach heights of up to 10,000 feet.
  • Known for their playful nature, they can often be seen rolling around and playing with each other.

The Panda Diet

  • Giant Pandas are primarily herbivores, with bamboo making up about 99% of their diet. They consume large quantities of bamboo daily, which is low in nutrients, so they need to eat a lot to meet their energy needs.
  • Giant Pandas also eat other plants, fruits, and on rare occasions, small animals. However, bamboo is their main, and favourite, food source.
  • Due to their specialized diet, Giant Pandas evolved unique adaptations, such as strong jaws and teeth to help them chew through tough bamboo stalks. They also have a special digestive system that allows them to extract the maximum nutrients from bamboo.

Habitat & Breeding Program

  • Information about the Panda habitat and the neccessity of captivity.
  • Giant Pandas are native to the mountainous regions of central China, where they inhabit dense bamboo forests. These forests provide the necessary food and shelter for their survival.
  • Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, Giant Pandas are classified as vulnerable
  • Conservation efforts include creating reserves and breeding programs. These programs aim to increase the population of Giant Pandas in captivity and eventually reintroduce them into the wild.
